Electric vehicle infrastructure incentives are available to those who purchase a new EverCharge EV Charging Station. Several states offer additional incentives, often taking the form of a rebate. Rebates can be claimed immediately after purchase, while tax credits are claimed when filing income taxes.

Alabama
Commercial customers may receive $500 for qualified commercial electric vehicle supply equipment (EVSE).
California
Up to $750/charger installed (does not cover install costs).
Bay Area: Up to $3,000/charger with a minimum of 4 chargers installed.
Colorado
Grants up to 80% of the station costs up to $3,260.
Delaware
$500 dollars for every charger installed.
Georgia
Tax incentives of 10% of the cost of the charger, up to $2,500.
Illinois
50% of the cost of equipment and installation up to $3,750-7,500 depending on number of stations.
Indiana
Up to $1,650 to purchase and install residential EVSE, as well as free plug-in electric vehicle charging during off-peak hours.
Louisiana
$750 toward qualified PEV purchases (funds limited).
Maryland
Income tax credit equal to 20% of the cost of qualified charger, may not exceed $400.
Massachusetts
Up to $13,500 for the purchase or lease of Level 2 EVSE.
Michigan
Up to $2,500 to residential customers.
Missouri
Tax credit of 20% of the costs associated with the purchase and installation of a charger up to $1,500 for individuals and $20,000 for businesses.
New York
Tax credit is available for 50% of the cost infrastructure, up to $5,000.
Oklahoma
75% of the cost of installing commercial alternative fueling infrastructure.
Oregon
Residents: Tax credit for 25% of infrastructure project costs, up to $750.
Businesses: 35% of eligible costs for infrastructure projects.
Texas
Residents of Austin, TX are eligible for a rebate of 50% of the cost.
Utah
Provides grants and loans to assist businesses and government entities.
Washington
Provides rate of return on EVSE installed for the benefit of ratepayers up to 2%, Puget Sound Energy (PSE) provides $500/charger rebate
Washington DC
Grants tax credit for 50% of labour and equipment, up to $1,000
