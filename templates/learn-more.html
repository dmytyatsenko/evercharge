Know Your Rights

Many states prohibit denying residents the right to charge their electric vehicle at home (and many more states are likely to pass legislation this year). The following states currently have legislation in place:

California

On February 29, 2012 the Governor of California signed into law Senate Bill 880, to take effect immediately. The bill guarantees owners the right to install and use an electric vehicle charging station in their own parking space. If it is impossible or unreasonably expensive to install an EV charging station in your own space, you can install your charger in a common area for exclusive use.

Evercharge already provides service for several buildings in California, contact us today to get started.

Colorado

On May 5, 2013 SB 13-126 was signed into law in Colorado. HOAs cannot prohibit an owner from installing an EV charging station in their garage stall, parking space, or carport, nor can they charge a fee for the placement of the system.

We are experienced lobbying to HOAs and Property Owners, contact us today and let us handle the process for you.

Hawaii

As of June 30, 2013, ยง196-7.5 is in effect in the state of Hawaii. The legislation allows residents of multi-family homes to install an EV charging station in or near their parking space.

If you live in Hawaii and are interested in EV charging in your condo or apartment, let Evercharge handle the details for you.

Oregon

Oregon law dictates that "an association of unit owners may not prohibit installation or use of a charging station," but they can require an application and review process, with a potential reasonable fee for costs incurred during the review.

Evercharge currently provides service in Oregon, and can help you navigate the application and review if necessary - Get a quote today.
